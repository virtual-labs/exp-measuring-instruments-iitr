CRO (Cathode Ray Oscilloscope )
  • A cathode ray oscilloscope (CRO) is a device that converts electrical signals into an active graphical representation on a fluorescent screen.

  • A cathode ray oscilloscope is a very fast X-Y plotter that can displays voltage waveforms on a screen, allowing users to observe the shape, amplitude, frequency, and other characteristics of the signal.

    • Operation:

    1. Signal Input: The electrical signal is fed into the oscilloscope through input channels.

    2. Deflection: The signal is used to control the vertical deflection of the electron beam, while the time base controls the horizontal movement.

    3. Display: The beam creates a trace on the phosphor screen that represents the signal waveform. The waveform’s shape and characteristics are shown based on the signal’s voltage over time.
