WATTMETER

A wattmeter is an essential instrument in electrical engineering used to measure the power consumption of an electrical circuit. It specifically measures the real power (in watts) that is being consumed by an electrical load, which is a crucial parameter in both domestic and industrial applications for understanding energy usage and efficiency.

Working Principle

A wattmeter typically comprises two coils: the current coil and the potential (or voltage) coil.

1. Current Coil: This coil is connected in series with the load and carries the load current. It experiences the full current of the circuit.

2. Potential Coil: This coil is connected in parallel with the load and measures the voltage across the load. It is designed to handle the full voltage of the circuit but carries only a small current due to its high impedance.

The interaction between the magnetic fields produced by these coils generates a deflecting torque proportional to the product of the current and voltage, which is directly related to the power being consumed.
