GALVANOMETER

A galvanometer is an instrument used to detect and measure small electric currents. It operates on the principle of electromagnetism and was a crucial tool in the early days of electrical science for understanding and measuring current flow.

Principle of Operation

A galvanometer works based on the interaction between a magnetic field and an electric current. The core components include a coil of wire, a magnetic field (typically provided by a permanent magnet), and a pointer or needle that indicates the measurement.

When an electric current passes through the coil, it generates a magnetic field around it. This magnetic field interacts with the field of the permanent magnet, creating a torque that causes the coil to rotate. The rotation is proportional to the current passing through the coil. This movement is then translated into a readable output via a pointer attached to the coil, which moves over a calibrated scale.
